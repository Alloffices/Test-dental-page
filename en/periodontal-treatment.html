Periodontal treatment

Gingivitis

The most common inflammatory disease of the body is gingivitis, i.e. the inflammation of the gum tissue. Its symptoms are:

  • purple and swollen gums
  • bleeding gums
  • attachment loss (pockets between tooth and gum)

The overwhelming majority of gingivitis occurences is caused by bacterial plaques that are accumulating in the small gaps between the teeth, in the gingival grooves. Gingivitis can be treated and prevented through proper oral hygiene including daily brushing, flossing and antibacterial mouth wash.

Periodontitis

Untreated gingivitis is sure to progress into the deeper lying tissues causing periodontitis, i.e. the inflammation of the supporting tissues of the teeth. Its symptoms are the following:

  • purple and swollen gums
  • deep and often bleeding pockets between the teeth and the gums
  • gingival recession, resulting in apparent lengthening of the teeth
  • loose teeth in the later stages

Regarding the complexity of the disease, the treatment is carried out in several stages within a matter of weeks, sometimes even months.

The primary cause of periodontitis is also poor oral hygiene which leads to the accumulation of bacterial plaques on the surfaces of the teeth. The continuous inflammation destroys the supporting gingival fibers as well as the jaw bone eventually resulting in the loss of the teeth. The exact course of the disease, however, is often modified by genetic, hormonal or systemic factors.

Course of treatment

  • Pre-treatment The first step is cleaning the teeth and establishing a satisfactory oral hygiene based on the dentist's instructions. The patient has to be able to keep clean his teeth, interdental spaces and gingival pockets using the tools and methods recommended by the dental professional.
  • Periodontal treatment The treatment itself involves complete mechanical removal of tartar and bacterial plaques from below the gum line. The procedure is called scaling and root planing. It is performed under local anaesthesia and requires multiple visits.
    In more advanced stages, surgical therapy might be necessary. There are many surgical approaches sharing the same goal: to reduce pockets as much as possible, thus providing access and making it possible for the patient to maintain good oral hygiene.
  • Maintenance Upon completion of a successful treatment an ongoing periodontal maintenance is indispensable. This must be based on the patient's active cooperation since maintaining an excellent personal oral hygiene and regular checkups and cleanings every couple of months are equally important.
