Dental restorations

Heavily damaged or missing teeth can be replaced in countless ways using different techniques. They all serve the same purpose though: to reinstate the original function and appearance of the teeth.

more

Dental implants

Implants have been used in dentistry for many years now. It is a well established technique that opened a whole new world of prosthodontic treatments thereby increasing the number of available treatment options for the patients.

more

Fillings

Decayed, cracked or broken teeth must be repaired with fillings. The numerous methods and materials offer the best choice in every situation, be it a front tooth in the aesthetic zone or a back tooth in the molar region where great strength is required.

more

Dental surgery

Dental surgery deals with illnesses of the teeth, the jaw bone and the surrounding soft tissues that require surgical treatment. These range from simple tooth extraction to the more complex ones like osteotomy or root tip resection.

more

Periodontal treatment

In lack of treatment, the inflammation of the gum (gingivitis) may turn into periodontitis causing the destruction of the tissues surrounding and supporting the teeth. Only a systematic periodontal treatment can prevent the progression of the disease.

more

Cosmetic dentistry

Cosmetic dentistry deals with problems and treatments that do not affect the well-being of the teeth but improve their appearance.

more

Oral hygiene

Personal oral hygiene is based on regular tooth brushing complemented with further measures - sometimes even involving the dentist if needed. It's more than just a cosmetic effort; it's the foundation for healthy teeth.
