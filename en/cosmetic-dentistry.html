Cosmetic dentistry

Bleaching

Over time, teeth get discoloured due to various factors. Every food contains dyes that may be deposited in the tooth enamel, particularly to blame are drinks such as coffee, tea, red wine, cola as well as tobacco. Modern dentistry, however, offers several methods to brighten up the darkened teeth.

  • Home bleaching Bleaching is carried out at home. The patient is given a plastic tooth splint by the dentist on which he applies the whitening gel and puts on his/her teeth every night.
  • In-office bleaching Since this procedure is done in the practice under the dentist's supervision, the gums will be protected best, and the bleaching process accelerated. It works with a high concentration of urea, which ensures a faster and more effective whitening. In both cases the bleaching is preceded by a professional tooth cleaning and followed by the abandonment of smoking and the consumption of the above beverages for about 1-2 weeks.
  • Walking bleach Root canal treated teeth often take on a grayish color with time. These teeth can also be brightened individually with a strong bleach from the inside. The bleaching gel is placed in the tooth for a few days, and the process can even be carried out again if so desired.

After bleaching, a temporary sensitivity of the teeth may occur, which usually sets in a short time. The method is not harmful to the teeth, it may therefore be repeated several times - within reasonable intervals.

Veneer

Veneers are wafer-thin ceramic shells for the anterior teeth. They can also be regarded as a special type of ceramic crowns because they only cover the visible outer surface and the cutting edge of the teeth. They look very natural, and only a thin layer of tooth enamel needs to be removed during preparation. This is also their drawback, though: they are very fragile. However, there are cases where they should be taken into consideration:

  • Replacement of (not too large) fillings
  • Discolouration which can not be removed by bleaching
  • Restoration of broken teeth
  • Elimination of deeper lying defects in the tooth enamel
  • Correction of the shape of teeth
  • Correction of the axis of teeth

Dental jewelry

Nowadays there is often a demand for dental ornamentation. We use Skyce® dental jewelry made of rhinestone. These can simply be glued to the desired location and later completely removed without leaving any damage to the tooth.
