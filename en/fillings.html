Fillings 

Direct fillings

After removing the decayed parts the dentist places the filling into the cavity while it is soft, and the material sets hard quickly. Direct fillings are therefore made relatively easily in just one visit. However, they don't live up to the expectations in every aspect.

  • Composite Dental composites, also calles white fillings, are a mixture of plastic resin and powdered glass or ceramics. They are put into the prepared tooth in a soft state and then hardened by light exposure.
    advantages disadvantages
    immediate load possible marginal gap between filling and tooth
    no thermal sensitivity limited durability
    (initially) good aesthetics discolouration
    low price possible source of allergy
  • Amalgam This is still the most extensively used filling material in the world. It is composed of a powdered silver-tin-zinc alloy which is then mixed with mercury and jammed into the tooth where it sets hard in a few hours.
    advantages disadvantages
    longevity unattractive appearance
    antibacterial effect initial thermal sensitivity
    great strength may cause oral galvanism
    easy application mercury leakage
    low price  

Amalgam toxicity is a controversial and highly debated issue. While not every amalgam filling should be removed without any deliberation, there are many good reasons to replace them with other materials of superior quality.

Indirect fillings

Indirect fillings, or inlays, are made in the dental laboratory, and the procedure takes two visits. The dentist makes an impression from the prepared tooth first. Based on this impression the dental technician fabricates the inlay which is then placed and bonded to the tooth by the dentist during a second visit.

  • Gold inlay Gold is one of the oldest and still the best filling material, considered even today as the benchmark. Due to its favourable physical properties and its antibacterial effect gold inlays have an excellent durability. They are somewhat expensive, yet very economical on the long term as it is not uncommon for them to last several decades.
    advantages disadvantages
    high precision high price
    longevity initial thermal sensitivity
    biocompatibility appearance unacceptable for some
    antibacterial effect  
  • Porcelain inlay Inlays made of ceramic show high durability and outstanding aesthetics at the same time. This is the main reason for being the most popular choice of long-term solutions.
    advantages disadvantages
    excellent aesthetics relatively high costs
    biocompatibility risk of breakage
    high durability
